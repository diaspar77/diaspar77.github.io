Note on Ontology

We consider a community of monads each having their own phenomenological sphere. To this end we consider the class of first-order theories $T_S$ over a first-order languages $S$. First for simplicity suppose that $S$ is fixed. Each monad has so many modes which correspond to (generally incomplete) theories $T_\alpha$ indexed by some ordinal $\omega$. We suppose that $\alpha < \beta$ implies that $T_\alpha \subset T_\beta$. We suppose that at the limit $T_\omega$ is complete.
A world is a theory $W$ which specifies how monads in given modes coexist on a certain plane. Worlds are also indexed by ordinals. The pleroma consists of complete theories.

Some problems with Antinatalism

Intelligent and compassionate people are more likely than less intelligent and compassionate people to harken to antinalist arguments and abstain from procreation out of compassion. This will result in the long run in a genetic deterioration of mankind as a whole and hence in the increase of suffering and the increase in procreation.

We do not know the nature of the relationship between the mind and the body. Parents do not "create" their offsprings but merely are causal factors in the origin of the physical organism. Hence in order to be certain that a certain life better had never been we would have to know about the possible pre-natal and post-mortem existence an individual consciousness. But we do not.

On the other hand the decrease of the human population to 18th-century levels is a moral and ecological imperative. It is also imperative to eradicate the culture of procreation that permeates society. It is an ideal that there be a decrease in the human population but an increase in quality. There are however no clear methods to achieve this. We can always cry "education !". Education based on the importance of compassion/empathy and the love of learning
